Sunita Williams

Expedition 14/15 (2006-2007)

Sunita Williams launched on Expedition 14/15 in December 2006 aboard Space Shuttle Atlantis, spending 195 days on the ISS. She set a record for the longest spaceflight by a female astronaut at the time. Williams conducted crucial spacewalks to install new solar arrays and upgrade the ISS’s power systems. Her efforts significantly enhanced the station's capabilities and contributed valuable data through scientific experiments. Her time in space was also marked by extensive public outreach, inspiring global audiences.

Expedition 32/33 (2012-2013)

Returning to the ISS in July 2012 for Expedition 32/33, Sunita Williams spent 127 days in orbit. She performed multiple spacewalks for the installation and maintenance of essential equipment, ensuring smooth station operations. Her work supported various scientific experiments and station upgrades, advancing our understanding in multiple fields. Williams also engaged in public outreach, sharing her space experiences and fostering interest in space exploration. Her efforts continued to inspire and educate the public about space science.

Expedition 35 (2013)

During Expedition 35, which began in March 2013, Sunita Williams further contributed to the ISS through diverse scientific experiments and critical maintenance tasks. Her expertise was vital for performing complex repairs and upgrades, enhancing the station’s research capabilities. Williams’s commitment extended to public outreach, where she promoted STEM education and inspired future generations. Her achievements in space continued to highlight her dedication to both scientific advancement and public engagement.

He's my inspiration because...

  • Sunita Williams holds records for the most spacewalks by a woman and the longest cumulative spacewalk time. Her achievements exemplify resilience and a pioneering spirit in space exploration.
  • Before becoming an astronaut, Williams served as a skilled helicopter pilot and test pilot in the U.S. Navy. Her dedication to service underscores her commitment to contributing to her country and the world.
  • Williams completed the Boston Marathon in space, running on a treadmill aboard the ISS. This incredible feat showcases her physical and mental strength, pushing the boundaries of human capability.
  • As a female astronaut of Indian-American descent, Sunita Williams is a role model for women and minorities in STEM. Her achievements inspire young people to pursue their dreams in science and technology.
